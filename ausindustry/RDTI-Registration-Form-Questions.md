# R&D Tax Incentive — Application questions

> **About this guidance.** Provides an overview of how to access and progress through the online application to register activities for the R&D Tax Incentive. Broken down into sections matching each page of the online application.

> **Important note re: file naming.** Existing repo file `RDTI-Application-Guide.md` is *CAPIX-specific notes/strategy*, not the official questions list. This file contains the official AusIndustry question-and-guidance text from `RDTI Registration Form List of Questions PDF.pdf`.

---

## Section: Income Period

### Does the company follow a standard income period (01 July to 30 June)?
- **Style:** Radio button
- **Options:** Yes, No
- **Guidance:** This should be the income period on the company's income tax return submitted to the Australian Taxation Office.

### Which Australian Taxation Office approved income period does the company follow?
- **Style:** Radio button
- **Options:** Substituted accounting period (SAP); Transitional accounting period
- **Shown if:** "No" selected for *Does the company follow a standard income period*.
- **Guidance:** Only companies with a substituted accounting period approved by the ATO may register activities for a non-standard income period.

### Which income period does the company follow?
- **Style:** Drop down list
- **Shown if:** "Substituted accounting period (SAP)" selected.
- **Guidance:** If your SAP ends on a date that is not listed, select the option that matches the month and year for your end date (e.g. SAP ending 5 April 2020 → select 30 April 2020).

### Start date of your transitional accounting period
- **Style:** Drop down list (Month/Year)
- **Shown if:** "Transitional accounting period" selected.

### End date of your transitional accounting period
- **Style:** Drop down list (Month/Year)
- **Shown if:** "Transitional accounting period" selected.

## Section: Registration type

### Is the company registered with the Australian Securities and Investments Commission?
- **Style:** Radio button
- **Options:**
  - Yes, under an Australian law
  - Yes, under foreign law that is an Australian resident for tax purposes
  - Yes, under foreign law AND is a resident of a country with which Australia has a double tax agreement, including a definition of 'permanent establishment' AND is carrying on business in Australia through a permanent establishment as defined in the double tax agreement
  - No, this company is not registered with ASIC
- **Guidance:** If your company is not registered with ASIC, it is not eligible for the R&D Tax Incentive.

## Section: Company details

### What country was the company incorporated in?
- **Style:** Drop down list
- **Shown if:** Company registered under foreign law.

### What date was the company registered with ASIC?
- **Style:** Date box (Day/Month/Year)
- **Guidance:** Find this in the ASIC register at ASIC Connect.

### What is the company's country of residence?
- **Style:** Drop down list
- **Shown if:** Company registered under foreign law AND is a resident of a country with which Australia has a double tax agreement and is carrying on business in Australia through a permanent establishment.

### Is the company the head of a consolidated or multiple entry consolidated group?
- **Style:** Radio button
- **Options:**
  - Yes
  - No, the company is not part of a consolidated or MEC group
  - No, the company is a subsidiary of a consolidated or MEC group
- **Guidance:** Only the head company of a consolidated or MEC group can apply to register R&D activities. The head company must register R&D activities performed by any member of the group.

### Is the company controlled by one or more tax exempt entities?
- **Style:** Radio button
- **Options:** Yes, No
- **Guidance:** Consider whether one or more exempt entities (and/or affiliates) hold either ≥50% voting power or the right to receive ≥50% of distributed income/capital.

### What percentage control is held by tax exempt entities?
- **Style:** Percentage (1–100 inclusive)
- **Shown if:** "Yes" to *controlled by one or more tax exempt entities*.
- **Guidance:** Companies controlled by exempt entities cannot claim the refundable offset but can claim the non-refundable offset instead.

### Does the company have an Ultimate Holding Company?
- **Style:** Radio button
- **Options:** Yes, No

### Ultimate Holding Company details (if Yes)
- **Sub-questions:**
  - What country was the Ultimate Holding Company incorporated in?
  - What is the Ultimate Holding Company's ABN or ACN? *(Australian-incorporated only)*
  - What is the Ultimate Holding Company Name? *(non-Australian-incorporated only)*

### Indigenous ownership and control
- **Sub-questions:**
  - Is the company Indigenous owned (≥51% members/proprietors are Indigenous)?
  - Is the company Indigenous controlled (≥51% board/management are Indigenous)?
- **Guidance:** Required for policy/program development, review and evaluation.

### Which industry does the company mostly operate in?
- **Style:** Drop down lists × 2 (ANZSIC Division and ANZSIC Class)
- **Guidance:** Select the ANZSIC Division/Class that best describes the company's main business activity. For consolidated/MEC groups, use the main activity for the majority of subsidiaries.

### Did you receive advice from an R&D Consultant?
- **Style:** Radio button — Yes, No
- **Guidance:** This section collects details of R&D Consultants who are not Registered Tax Agents.

## Section: Application inclusions

### This application will include:
- **Style:** Check box (select one or more)
- **Options:**
  - Activities with an advance or overseas finding
  - Expenditure paid via a levy to a Research Service Provider
  - Activities conducted by a Research Service Provider
  - Activities conducted by a Cooperative Research Centre
  - Activities conducted by another research organisation
  - Activities conducted under another collaborative agreement
  - None of the above
- **Guidance:**
  - **Advance and overseas findings** — provide certainty about eligibility; mandatory for any overseas activities being registered.
  - **Research Service Providers** — provide scientific/technical expertise to perform R&D on behalf of other companies. Engaging an RSP allows claiming an offset even if total claim is less than the usual $20,000 expenditure threshold.
  - **Cooperative Research Centres** — bring researchers, industry and government together for industry-led collaborative research.

### Levy-collecting Research Service Provider details
- **Shown if:** "Expenditure paid via a levy to a Research Service Provider" selected.
- **Sub-questions:**
  - Name of the Research Service Provider
  - Total levy paid for selected income period
  - Proportion of the levy used for providing R&D services (%)
  - Levy used for providing R&D services *(auto-calculated)*
  - Total levy allocated to core activities
  - Total levy allocated to supporting activities

### Research Service Provider details (non-levy)
- **Shown if:** "Activities conducted by a Research Service Provider" selected.
- **Sub-questions:** Name of RSP; Amount paid in income period

### Cooperative Research Centre details
- **Shown if:** "Activities conducted by a Cooperative Research Centre" selected.
- **Sub-questions:** Name of CRC; Amount paid in income period

### Will the company be including activities that are excluded from being a core activity in this application?
- **Style:** Radio button — Yes, as supporting activities; No
- **Guidance:** s 355-25(2) ITAA 1997 lists activities that cannot be core R&D activities. They may still be supporting R&D activities **only if undertaken for the dominant purpose of supporting core R&D activities** (and where they produce goods/services or directly relate to producing goods/services).

### Is this application for: (consolidated head only)
- **Style:** Radio button
- **Options:**
  - The head company only
  - Subsidiary members only
  - Both the head company and subsidiary members
- **Shown if:** "Yes" to *head of consolidated or MEC group*.

### Subsidiary member details
- **Shown if:** "Subsidiary members only" or "Both …" above.
- **Sub-questions:** Subsidiary ABN; Subsidiary Name; Subsidiary website (optional)

## Section: Employees

### How many employees did the company have *across all companies* at the end of [Income period]?
- **Style:** Number field
- **Guidance:** Total employees on payroll at end of income period (working directors, partners, proprietors, full time, part time, casual). For consolidated groups, total for the entire group.

### How many employees *across all companies* were engaged in the R&D activities included in this application?
- **Style:** Number field (FTE)
- **Guidance:** Full-time equivalent. Pro-rata part-time staff (e.g. half-time = 0.5). For consolidated groups, total FTE for all relevant subsidiaries that performed R&D in this application. Estimates only acceptable if actual numbers are not available.

## Section: Finance

### For your selected income period, what was the company's taxable income or loss *across all companies*?
- **Style:** Currency field
- **Guidance:** Losses shown as negative figures. For consolidated/MEC groups: the group is a single entity for income tax purposes.

### For your selected income period, what was the company's aggregated turnover?
- **Style:** Currency field
- **Guidance:** Sum of annual turnover for the company + any connected entity + any affiliated entity (excluding intra-group dealings). Annual turnover = total ordinary income derived in the year (excluding GST). For non-grouped companies, aggregated turnover = annual turnover.

### For your selected income period, how much revenue did the company earn *across all companies* from export sales?
- **Style:** Currency field
- **Guidance:** Total revenue from export sales reported in the company's BAS to the ATO. Sum the individual export-sale amounts across the year's BAS submissions.

## Section: Projects

### Name for this project
- **Style:** Text field
- **Guidance:** If you have registered this project before, please use the same name.

### Project reference description (optional)
- **Style:** Text field
- **Guidance:** Optional internal reference field.

### Does this project include activities covered by advance or overseas findings?
- **Style:** Radio button — Yes, No
- **Shown if:** "Activities with an advance or overseas finding" selected on Application inclusions.
- **Guidance:** A finding attaches to the described activities and is limited to the particular company. If the activity actually undertaken differs materially, it may still be eligible — for Australian activities under self-assessment; for overseas activities a separate overseas finding is required. Companies must register all activities before the deadline including any overseas activities where a finding decision is still pending.

### What are the certificate reference numbers for the findings?
- **Style:** Text field
- **Shown if:** "Yes" to project includes findings.

### What is the expected duration of this project?
- **Style:** Date boxes (Month/Year start and end)

### How much is expected to be spent over the life of this project?
- **Style:** Currency field
- **Guidance:** Reasonable estimate for all years and all companies. Include overseas expenditure (which will need an overseas finding) where applicable.

### What are the objectives of this project?
- **Style:** Text field
- **Field capacity:** 1,000 characters
- **Guidance:** Project-level objectives may be described fairly broadly and can include both R&D and commercial aims.

### For the selected income period, how much was spent on feedstock inputs?
- **Style:** Currency field
- **Guidance:** Feedstock inputs are goods/materials transformed or processed during R&D activities in producing tangible products. Refer to the ATO website for further information.

### For the selected income period, where in Australia did the company conduct most of the R&D activities in this project?
- **Style:** Post code field

### Which field of research best describes the majority of R&D activities in this project?
- **Style:** Drop down lists × 2 (ANZSRC Division, ANZSRC Group)
- **Guidance:** ANZSRC = Australian and New Zealand Standard Research Classification. Full list available from the ABS.

## Section: Core Activities

> **Legal definition (s 355-25(1) ITAA 1997).** Core R&D activities are experimental activities:
> (a) whose outcome cannot be known or determined in advance on the basis of current knowledge, information or experience, but can only be determined by applying a systematic progression of work that:
>   (i) is based on the principles of established science; and
>   (ii) proceeds from hypothesis to experiment, observation and evaluation, and leads to logical conclusions; and
> (b) that are conducted for the purpose of generating new knowledge (including new knowledge in the form of new or improved materials, products, devices, processes or services).

### Name for this core activity
- **Style:** Text field

### Which project is this core activity related to?
- **Style:** Drop down list
- **Options:** Projects entered in the previous step.

### Does this core activity commence after the end of your income period for this application?
- **Style:** Radio button — Yes, No
- **Guidance:** If yes, you'll need to provide the title, start/end date, brief description, and intended new knowledge.

### Enter the start and end dates for this core activity
- **Style:** Date boxes (Month/Year)
- **Guidance:** Start/end must fall within the related project's dates.

### A brief description of the activity
- **Style:** Text field
- **Field capacity:** 1,000 characters
- **Shown if:** "Yes" to *commences after end of income period*.

### Will you conduct this core activity for a substantial purpose of generating new knowledge?
- **Style:** Radio button — Yes, No
- **Shown if:** "Yes" to *commences after end of income period*.
- **Guidance on "new knowledge":** New to the world (not available publicly or reasonably accessibly when activities were conducted). Can be general or applied; theoretical or practical; new or improved material/product/device/process/service.
- **Guidance on "substantial purpose":** Doesn't have to be the *sole* purpose. Must be a substantial purpose at the time you undertake the activity.

### What new knowledge is this core activity intended to produce?
- **Style:** Text field
- **Field capacity:** 1,000 characters
- **Shown if:** "Yes" to *substantial purpose of generating new knowledge*.

> **The following questions are only shown if "No" is selected for "Does this core activity commence after the end of your income period?"**

### Was some or all of this core activity conducted by a Research Service Provider or Cooperative Research Centre?
- **Style:** Radio button
- **Options:** Yes, a non-levy collecting RSP; Yes, a levy collecting RSP; Yes, a CRC; No
- **Shown if:** levy/RSP/CRC selected on Application inclusions.

### Research Service Provider details (per core activity)
- **Sub-questions:**
  - Which RSP conducted some or all of this activity?
  - Brief description of the services provided by the RSP

### Cooperative Research Centre details (per core activity)
- **Sub-questions:**
  - Which CRC conducted some or all of this activity?
  - Brief description of the services provided by the CRC

> **The following core-activity questions are NOT required if "Yes, a levy collecting RSP" was selected for the prior question.**

### For your selected income period, what was the estimated expenditure for this core activity?
- **Style:** Currency field
- **Guidance:** Reasonable estimate. Include expenditure conducted by the company plus contracted expenditure to RSPs/CRCs (if any).

### What was the hypothesis?
- **Style:** Text field
- **Field capacity:** 4,000 characters
- **Guidance:** Your hypothesis is your idea or proposed explanation for how you could achieve a particular result and why that result may or may not be achievable. May be a single statement or several. Explain (a) what result you aim to achieve, and (b) how and why you think you can achieve it, informed by your background research.

### Did you conduct this core activity for a substantial purpose of generating new knowledge?
- **Style:** Radio button — Yes, No
- **Guidance on "new knowledge":** Must be new to the world; not publicly/reasonably accessibly available when activities conducted.

### What new knowledge was this core activity intended to produce?
- **Style:** Text field
- **Field capacity:** 1,000 characters
- **Shown if:** "Yes" to *substantial purpose of generating new knowledge*.

### How did the company determine that the outcome could not be known in advance?
- **Style:** Check box (select all that apply)
- **Options:**
  - There was no applicable information in scientific, technical, or professional literature or patents
  - Experts in the field provided advice that there wasn't a solution that could be applied
  - There wasn't a way to adapt solutions from other companies in, and out of, Australia
  - Other
  - The company did not look into existing knowledge
- **Guidance:** A competent professional must not be able to know/determine the outcome based on knowledge/info/experience that is publicly available or reasonably accessible anywhere in the world.

### Please explain what sources were investigated, what information was found, and why a competent professional could not have known or determined the outcome in advance.
- **Style:** Text field
- **Field capacity:** 1,000 characters
- **Shown if:** any of the first four options selected above.
- **Guidance:** Information that is not reasonably accessible is not available (e.g. trade secrets held by competitors). You may review scientific/technical/professional literature, do internet searches, conduct patent searches, seek expert advice. A competent professional has knowledge, experience, qualifications, keeps up to date with developments, and has access to internet/journals/other professionals worldwide.

### Please explain why the company did not search existing knowledge.
- **Style:** Text field
- **Field capacity:** 1,000 characters
- **Shown if:** "The company did not look into existing knowledge" selected.
- **Guidance:** AusIndustry expects you to search worldwide for an existing way to achieve your outcome before you start. Even if you/your team is an expert, you must research other sources.

### What was the experiment and how did it test the hypothesis?
- **Style:** Text field
- **Field capacity:** 4,000 characters
- **Guidance:** A scientific procedure undertaken to test your hypothesis, observe what happens, compare to expectations and prior experiments. Either supportive or contradictory results can still be eligible R&D.

### How did you evaluate or plan to evaluate results from your experiment?
- **Style:** Text field
- **Field capacity:** 4,000 characters
- **Guidance:** Analyse how/why something occurs and explain insights through descriptions. Use established techniques for numerical data. Evaluate causal relationships between varied/held-constant/measured parameters.

### If you reached conclusions from your experiments in the selected income period, describe those conclusions.
- **Style:** Text field
- **Field capacity:** 4,000 characters
- **Guidance:** Form logical conclusions about why results support the hypothesis or not. A logical conclusion may be that you need to investigate different solutions and test a new hypothesis. New experiments may be similar enough to belong to the same core activity, or different enough to be a new core activity.

### What evidence did the company keep about this core activity?
- **Style:** Check box
- **Options:**
  - Evidence of searches or enquiries you made to find current knowledge
  - Evidence to show that you could only determine the outcome by conducting experiments as part of a systematic progression of work
  - Evidence of your hypothesis and design of your experiments
  - Documented results and evaluation of your experiments
  - Other
  - The company did not keep records
- **Guidance:** Records should reflect your purposes at the time you started activities. Evidence must show how you conduct/plan to conduct core activities that are based on principles of established science; whose outcome cannot be known or determined in advance; whose outcome can only be determined by applying a systematic progression of work (hypothesis → experiment → observation → evaluation → logical conclusions); for the purpose of generating new knowledge; and that are not excluded from being core R&D activities.

### Please describe the other evidence
- **Style:** Text field
- **Field capacity:** 100 characters
- **Shown if:** "Other" selected above.

## Section: Supporting activities

> **Legal definition (s 355-30 ITAA 1997).**
> (1) Supporting R&D activities are activities directly related to core R&D activities.
> (2) However, if an activity:
>   (a) is an activity referred to in subsection 355-25(2); or
>   (b) produces goods or services; or
>   (c) is directly related to producing goods or services;
> the activity is a supporting R&D activity only if it is undertaken for the **dominant purpose** of supporting core R&D activities.

### Name for this supporting activity
- **Style:** Text field
- **Guidance:** If previously registered, use the same name.

### Was some or all of this supporting activity conducted by a Research Service Provider or Cooperative Research Centre?
- **Style:** Check box
- **Options:** Yes, a non-levy RSP; Yes, a levy collecting RSP; Yes, a CRC; No
- **Shown if:** levy/RSP/CRC selected on Application inclusions.

### Research Service Provider details (per supporting activity)
- **Sub-questions:** Which RSP; Brief description of services provided

### Cooperative Research Centre details (per supporting activity)
- **Sub-questions:** Which CRC; Brief description of services provided

### Briefly describe this supporting activity
- **Style:** Text field
- **Field capacity:** 1,000 characters
- **NOT required if:** "Yes, a levy collecting RSP" selected.
- **Guidance:** Explain what you did within the activity, the main actions/steps, scope and timing.

### Which core activities are supported by this supporting activity?
- **Style:** Drop down list (multi-select)
- **Options:** Core activities added to the application.

### When was this supporting activity conducted?
- **Style:** Date boxes (Month/Year)
- **Guidance:** Start/end must fall within the related project's dates AND within the income period for this application.

> **The following supporting-activity questions are NOT required if "Yes, a levy collecting RSP" was selected.**

### How did this activity directly support the core activities?
- **Style:** Text field
- **Guidance:** Identify the core R&D activity to which the supporting activity directly relates, and describe how the activity supported any/all of:
  - the development or refinement of a hypothesis
  - the conduct of an experiment
  - the observation and evaluation of the results of an experiment
  - the drawing of subsequent logical conclusions

### For the selected income period, what was the estimated expenditure for this supporting activity?
- **Style:** Currency field
- **Guidance:** Reasonable estimate. Include expenditure by the company + contracted expenditure to RSPs/CRCs (if any).

### Did this activity produce a good or a service, or is it directly related to producing a good or a service?
- **Style:** Radio button — Yes, No
- **Guidance:** For supporting activities that produce or directly relate to producing goods/services, you must be able to show they were conducted for the **dominant purpose** of supporting a core R&D activity (regardless of scale of manufacture or end user, and regardless of whether the service is provided to or received from another entity).

### Does this activity fall into one of the categories that exclude it from being a core activity?
- **Style:** Radio button — Yes, No
- **Shown if:** "Yes" to *Will the company be including activities that are excluded from being a core activity?* on Application inclusions.

### Was this activity undertaken for the dominant purpose of supporting the core activity?
- **Style:** Radio button — Yes, No
- **Shown if:** "Yes" was selected for either (a) *Does this activity fall into one of the categories that exclude it from being a core activity?* or (b) *Did this activity produce a good or service / directly relate to producing a good or service?*
- **Guidance on "dominant purpose":**
  - **What is dominant purpose?** Your prevailing or most influential purpose. Your main purpose of conducting your activities must be to support a core R&D activity.
  - **When do I need to have the dominant purpose?** Supporting R&D activities must directly relate to a core R&D activity to be eligible. You must conduct the relevant supporting activities (excluded; produce goods/services; directly relate to producing goods/services) for the dominant purpose of supporting your core activities.
  - **How do I assess my dominant purpose?** Consider:
    - how and when you conduct your activity, or establish the need to conduct it (usually when you plan or conduct your core R&D activity);
    - what other purposes you have, and whether one of the other purposes is more influential;
    - how you conduct your activity for the dominant purpose of supporting a core R&D activity (what does it involve, why is it needed for an element of your systematic progression of work?);
    - whether your activity goes beyond what you would have conducted if you did not conduct your core R&D activities.
